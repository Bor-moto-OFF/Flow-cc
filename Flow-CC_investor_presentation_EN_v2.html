The problem — and why removal matters

Cutting emissions is not enough. We must also remove CO₂ already in the air.

Climate math

To keep warming in check, the world needs both deep cuts and large‑scale CO₂ removal. It fills the gap where cuts are hard (aviation, agriculture, heavy industry) and cleans up past emissions.

Why DAC helps

Direct air capture works anywhere, near storage or fuel plants. It is measurable and auditable. It creates durable climate impact that buyers can trust.

Why cost < $100/t matters

Sub‑$100 unlocks mass buyers and public programs, allows city/enterprise budgets to scale, and makes millions of tons affordable every year.

What slows DAC today

High heat needs, big fans ($300–800/t), complex plants, long builds, land and energy friction.

Flow‑CC answer

Low‑power capture, solar heat for regeneration, and modular blocks. Built to scale fast with simple parts.

Our universal platform

One capture module and one simple chemistry (sodium carbonate/bicarbonate). Different regeneration blocks plug in by site.

Capture module

A 10×10×2 m falling film. Natural wind (~5 m/s) flows across it (~500 m³/s). CO₂ in air reacts with sodium carbonate.

24/7 buffer

At night the liquid stores CO₂ as bicarbonate. By day it is regenerated. Buffer size ~80–100 m³ per module.

Three regeneration options

A) Solar heating • B) Slag mineralization • C) Electro‑regeneration + e‑fuel. We can also mix them.

Three regeneration options — same module

A) Solar (South)

Three‑layer “solar packets” warm the liquid. CO₂ comes out and is collected. Very low power. Very simple.

  • Best in sunny regions
  • Capture cost: €90–120/t (5‑yr payback); path to €60–90/t
  • Revenue: removal credits (storage); e‑fuel later

B) Slag (Circular)

We contact the liquid with steel/cement slag. Calcium and magnesium form solid carbonates. The liquid is regenerated.

  • At or near plants and ports
  • Revenue: sell carbonates + removal credits
  • Often net‑positive after product sales

C) Electro + e‑fuel (North)

Electro‑regeneration and green hydrogen. CO₂ + H₂ → e‑fuel. Waste heat helps regeneration.

  • Best where power is cheap or negative at times
  • Value: energy security and storage of surplus wind/solar
  • Uses existing fuel infrastructure

Sizing and scalability

Per module

~2,000 tCO₂ per year. ~1,100–1,300 m² of solar field in sunny sites. Power ~0.10 MWh/t (pumps + compression).

Scale without limit

Add more capture modules. The only real limit is siting in windy regions for good airflow. We grow 10 → 20 → 50+ modules per site.

Mix & match

Use solar by day; add a small electro block for nights; add a slag unit for product revenue. Common buffer, compression, monitoring.
